I've been writing software for 20 years. Dozens of projects. Multiple businesses. And for most of that time, I've done it quietly.

No blog. No Twitter presence. No audience. Just heads-down shipping.

That changes now.

## Why Now?

Three reasons:

1. **I don't own my audience.** Everything I have lives on LinkedIn, X, and YouTube — platforms I don't control. If they change the algorithm tomorrow, I lose reach overnight.

2. **I have things worth sharing.** After two decades of building software, I've accumulated real lessons that could help other developers — especially solo founders.

3. **Building in public creates accountability.** When you share what you're working on, you're more likely to ship it.

## What I'll Write About

This blog will cover:

- **Building SaaS products solo** — architecture decisions, trade-offs, and real numbers
- **Ruby on Rails** — the framework I keep choosing after 20 years, and why
- **The business side** — pricing, distribution, validation, and what actually moves the needle
- **Tools and workflow** — what I use, what I've tried, what I've dropped

## The Stack

Everything I build runs on the same foundation:

```ruby
# The Jeremy Ward Stack™
Rails 8 + Hotwire + Tailwind + DaisyUI + Postgres + Kamal
```

It's boring. It works. That's the point.

## What This Site Is Built With

This site is itself a proof of concept. It's built with [RailsFoundry](https://railsfoundry.com) — my own Rails starter kit. Blog posts are markdown files. No CMS. No admin panel. Git push to publish.

The entire thing was built in a weekend. Ship fast, iterate later.
