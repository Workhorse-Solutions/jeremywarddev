Every couple of years, someone writes "Rails is dead" and the discourse machine spins up. Meanwhile, I keep shipping products with it.

Here's why I still choose Rails in 2024 — and why I think it's actually in the best position it's been in years.

## Rails 8 Changed the Game

Rails 8 shipped with answers to the last real complaints people had:

- **No more Redis dependency** — Solid Queue, Solid Cache, and Solid Cable replace Redis for jobs, caching, and WebSockets. One less service to manage.
- **Kamal 2** — Deploy anywhere with a single config file. No Heroku tax, no Kubernetes complexity.
- **Authentication generator** — Finally, built-in auth that's good enough for 90% of apps.

For a solo founder, this is huge. The operational overhead dropped significantly.

## The Solo Founder Argument

When you're building alone, your framework choice is a multiplier. Here's what I care about:

| Factor | Rails | Next.js | Laravel |
|--------|-------|---------|---------|
| Time to MVP | Days | Weeks | Days |
| Full-stack in one repo | ✅ | ❌ | ✅ |
| Background jobs built-in | ✅ | ❌ | ✅ |
| Deploy simplicity | Kamal | Vercel (vendor lock) | Forge |
| Hiring pool needed | Just me | Just me | Just me |

The "hiring pool" row is a joke, but it's real. As a solo founder, I pick the tool that lets *me* move fastest.

## The Boring Technology Thesis

Dan McKinley wrote about [choosing boring technology](https://mcfunley.com/choose-boring-technology) years ago, and it's still the best technology strategy advice I've seen.

Rails is boring. PostgreSQL is boring. Linux is boring. And boring is exactly what you want when you're trying to build a business, not a technology showcase.

```ruby
# This is all you need
rails new my_saas --database=postgresql
```

No webpack. No TypeScript configuration. No state management library decisions. Just start building the thing that matters — your product.

## What About [Insert Shiny Thing]?

- **"But React/Next.js..."** — Great for complex UIs. Overkill for most SaaS products. Hotwire handles 95% of what I need.
- **"But TypeScript..."** — I like type safety. I also like shipping. Ruby lets me ship faster.
- **"But serverless..."** — A $10/month VPS handles more traffic than most SaaS products will ever see.
- **"But microservices..."** — I'm one person. I'm going to run one service.

## The Proof Is in the Products

I'm not arguing theory. I have multiple products in production running Rails:

- **RailsFoundry** — Starter kit for new Rails apps
- **WorkhorseOps** — Trailer rental management
- **CoverText** — SMS for insurance agencies

All on the same stack. All shipping features. All maintained by one person.

That's the argument. Not benchmarks. Not blog posts. Working software.

## Bottom Line

If you're a solo founder building a SaaS product, Rails 8 is the best it's ever been. The ecosystem is mature, the deployment story is solved, and you can go from idea to production in a weekend.

Stop researching frameworks. Start building your product.
